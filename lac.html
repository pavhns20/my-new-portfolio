LUXURY ACCESS CLUB

About The Project

Over 4 weeks, myself and my fellow team member were charged with researching and validating the need for the new Luxury Access Club website.

We focused on designing a responsive website that will offer affordable, accessible luxury to users via single day pass access or membership option, so that they will be able to experience what luxury hotels have to offer without having to be an in-house guest.

Who is Our Audience?

With some interviews already completed, by our client it was evident that our target audience was people in their 30's- according to those findings, people in their 30's are far more likely to pay for comfort & luxury when travelling. One potential problem with these findings was that all of those interviewed had, in some way, a connection to our client so we felt that we needed to dive deeper to validate Luxury Access Club with no potential biases.

Key Insights From 14 Interviews Demand Side

Weighing Up The Competition

With a view to seeing what is available to users, we found there to be DayPass App & ResortPass App to be direct competitors. Both offer a similar product but neither offer a membership option as yet.

2 Journeys, Same Outcome

Our journey map shows the two different paths our users would travel whilst using the Luxury Access Club website. One purchasing membership, the other a single use day pass.
From our research, the main concern that people had was whether they might be treated differently or that they may have to wear a wristband-which would only compound their feelings of being treated differently to in-house guests.

Hypothesis

Demand Side Problem Statement

Our users need a way to access luxury hotels & resorts without having to book a room, so that they have the opportunity to experience luxury without the expense.

Demand Side Solution Statement

Design a mobile version website to offer users the opportunity to be able to purchase day-use passes & membership option to gain day access to hotels & resorts, taking advantage of the facilities without having to book a room.

Supply Side Problem Statement

Hotels need a way to occupy less used common areas in low season dips, which will aid in increasing revenue, whilst maintaining the exclusivity & security of guests & staff.

Supply Side Solution Statement

By doing so users will have their wishes fulfilled & will generate increased revenue for hotel & resort managers by making unused areas available to the public without affecting the experience of in-house guests.

At the beginning of this project we were told that "design would be considered a plus", but we were determined to make that happen. On presenting the hi-fi prototype, the client was impressed by what we were able to deliver within the designated timeframe.

Thank you for all that you've put together with this project. Also thank you for your professionalism with everyone that you interviewed on behalf of Luxury Access Club-everyone that I've spoken to were really happy to work with you & your chat.

Final Thoughts

Although a hi-fi fidelity prototype wasn't one of the requested deliverables, when we tested with SME's, it seemed that they really appreciated having a tangible product to interact with. I felt that by offering them the prototype to interact with they could actually understand how the Luxury Access Club website would work & this might not have been quite so clear when running usability testing with them if we only had a lo-fi prototype to offer them.
